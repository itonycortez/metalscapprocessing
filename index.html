What Are Metal Chip Shredders and Crushers?

Metal chip shredders and crushers are heavy-duty industrial machines that mechanically reduce the size and volume of metal chips, turnings, shavings, or scrap by cutting, tearing, or compressing actions. These processes transform bulky scrap into compact chips easier to transport, store, and recycle.

Key Features

  • High-torque shredders designed to handle tough, coiled, or tangled metal scrap

  • Crushers that compact and densify chips into smaller, denser forms

  • Capability to process ferrous and non-ferrous metals

  • Available as stand-alone units or integrated with chip processing systems

  • Automated designs for continuous, efficient operation

Benefits of Volume Reduction Using Shredders and Crushers

Significant Space Savings

Reducing scrap volume by up to 80-90% greatly decreases storage space requirements and transportation volumes, lowering associated costs.

Enhanced Scrap Handling and Safety

Smaller, uniform scrap chips reduce the risk of injury from sharp edges and make loading or conveying safer and more manageable.

Higher Scrap Value and Recycling Efficiency

Consistent, compact chip sizes improve scrap conveyor efficiency and downstream processing, boosting the material's recycling market price.

Fluid Recovery Facilitation

By preparing chips for fluid separation systems, shredders and crushers enable efficient cutting fluid recovery and reuse, reducing operational expenses.

Types of Metal Chip Shredders and Crushers

Horizontal Axis Shredders

Equipped with dual slow-speed shafts, they rip and tear long, curly turnings into manageable chips suitable for shredding at the source or integrated setups.

Vertical Crushers

Use hydraulic pressure to compress chips into solid blocks or briquettes, maximizing volume reduction and ease of transport.

Combined Shredding and Crushing Systems

Compact units combining shredding and crushing functions, often skid-mounted for small to medium operations, provide an all-in-one solution for volume reduction and fluid separation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What kinds of scrap can shredders and crushers process?

They handle ferrous and non-ferrous metals, including long turnings, curly chips, and shavings, both wet and dry.

How much volume reduction can these machines achieve?

Volume reductions of up to 80-90% are typical, depending on scrap type and machine settings.

Are shredders and crushers compatible with fluid recovery systems?

Yes, they prepare scrap for wringing and fluid separation, maximizing coolant recovery and reuse.

Can shredders operate as stand-alone units?

Yes, they can be used independently or integrated within comprehensive chip processing lines.

What maintenance is required?

Regular inspection and replacement of wear parts like blades and screens are necessary for optimal performance.
